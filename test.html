Gardens and gardeners come in all sizes, shapes, and needs. We may be short or tall, left-handed or right-handed. We may be starting a few planter boxes on the deck or growing entire rows of vegetables in the backyard. Thus, the tools that work for one person may not work for another. Complicating matters even more are tools that can serve multiple purposes. For example, some people might like a hand hoe for clearing weeds while others use it for creating furrows for bulbs. A weeder is made for pulling up taproots but its narrow head also makes it ideal for dibbling holes for seeds. What can be agreed on is that having the right tools for your particular needs is essential for your sanity and success in the garden. Use this shopping list as a guide of what might be useful when you hit the ground running. It’s best to “try on” tools in person so you can get a feel for what’s comfortable in your hand or proportionate to your height.
